DESCRIPTION:

Roti, also known as chapati, is a round flatbread native to the Indian subcontinent. It is widely consumed in many South Asian, Southeast Asian, Caribbean, and Southeast African countries.Roti is made from stoneground whole wheat flour, traditionally known as gehu ka atta, and water. These two simple ingredients are combined into a dough. The word “roti” is derived from the Sanskrit word roṭikā, meaning “bread” 1. Whether paired with cooked vegetables, curries, or used as a carrier for other dishes, roti remains a staple accompaniment in various cuisines.

There are many variants of rotis across the globe. Many cultures worldwide have their own variations of flatbreads similar to roti, from Africa to Oceania to the Malay Peninsula.Some of the variants of rotis are as follows. Chapati: Made of whole-wheat flour (atta), mixed into a dough with water, edible oil, and optional salt. It is cooked on a tava. Tandoori Roti: Cooked by sticking the flattened dough to the inside wall of a tandoor oven, where it bakes quickly at a high temperature.Paratha: A flaky, layered version of roti, often brushed with ghee or oil during cooking. Naan: Unlike roti, naan is yeast-leavened and typically thicker.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
  • Water (as needed for kneading)

RECIPE STEPS:

  1. In a mixing bowl, add the whole wheat flour.
  2. Gradually add water and knead the mixture until you have a smooth, elastic dough. Adjust the water as needed to achieve the right consistency.
  3. Divide the dough into small portions (about the size of a golf ball).
  4. Roll each portion into a round shape using a rolling pin (belan) on a floured surface (chakla).
  5. Heat a tawa (flat griddle) over medium heat.
  6. Place the rolled roti on the hot tawa and cook until bubbles appear on the surface.
  7. Flip the roti and cook the other side until it puffs up.
  8. Serve hot with your favorite curry or dal.
