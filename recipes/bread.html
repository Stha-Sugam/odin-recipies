DESCRIPTION:

Bread is a staple and one of the oldest foods prepared from a dough of flour(usually wheat) and water, usually by baking. Throughout recorded history and around the world, it has been an important part of many cultures' diet. Bread is a major source of complex carbohydrates and B vitamins (especially when whole wheat is used).Bread has not only sustained us physically but also played an essential role in both religious rituals and secular culture throughout human history

Evidence from 30,000 years ago in Europe and Australia revealed starch residue on rocks used for pounding plants. Primitive flatbreads may have been made by spreading starch extract from plant roots (such as cattails and ferns) on a flat rock and cooking it over a fire. Around 10,000 BC, with the dawn of the Neolithic age and the spread of agriculture, grains became the mainstay for making bread. Yeast spores are ubiquitous, so any dough left to rest leavens naturally.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 7 grams active dry yeast
  • 40 grams soft butter
  • 300 millimeters of warm water
  • 1 and half tablespoon Sugar
  • 1 and half tablespoon Salt
  • 2 tablespoons of Oil
  • 500 grams Flour (Wheat recommended)

RECIPE STEPS:

  1. Proof the yeast by combining it with warm water and a pinch of sugar or honey. Let it rest until foamy.
  2. Add the remaining sugar, salt, oil, and 3 cups of flour. Mix to form a smooth dough
  3. Knead the dough, let it rise, and shape it into loaves.
  4. Bake at 350°F (180°C) for about 30 minutes or until golden brown.
